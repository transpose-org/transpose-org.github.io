Governance

TRANSPOSE operates under an open governance model designed to ensure transparency, inclusivity, and sustainable development of our travel demand modeling ecosystem.

Mission & Scope

TRANSPOSE aims to advance travel demand modeling through open collaboration, reproducible research, and standardized benchmarking. We govern the development and maintenance of TDToolkit components, data access protocols, and community standards while fostering innovation in both classical and machine learning approaches to transportation modeling.

Organizational Structure

Steering Committee

The Steering Committee provides strategic direction and ensures alignment with the broader transportation research community. Members serve two-year terms and represent diverse institutions including universities, public agencies, and industry partners.

Maintainers

Component maintainers have commit access to specific TDToolkit repositories and are responsible for code review, release management, and technical decisions within their component's scope. Maintainers are nominated based on sustained contributions and technical expertise.

Working Groups

Data Working Group

Oversees data governance policies, access tier definitions, and privacy preservation standards.

Benchmarking Working Group

Maintains evaluation protocols, metric definitions, and ensures reproducibility across benchmarks.

Methods Working Group

Reviews new methodological contributions and maintains standards for model implementations.

Infrastructure Working Group

Manages CI/CD pipelines, documentation systems, and computational resource allocation.

Decision-Making Process

TRANSPOSE follows a lazy consensus model for most decisions. Proposals are considered approved if no objections are raised within the review period (typically 72 hours for minor changes, one week for significant changes).

  • Minor changes: Bug fixes, documentation updates, dependency updates
  • Significant changes: API modifications, new features, architectural decisions
  • Breaking changes: Require RFC (Request for Comments) process with two-week review period

When consensus cannot be reached, the Steering Committee may call for a vote among maintainers. Decisions require a simple majority, with the Committee chair holding a tie-breaking vote.

Release Cadence

TDToolkit components follow semantic versioning (MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH) with coordinated releases:

  • Patch releases: As needed for bug fixes and security updates
  • Minor releases: Monthly for new features and improvements
  • Major releases: Annually with potential breaking changes
  • Roadmap reviews: Quarterly to assess progress and adjust priorities

Security & Responsible Disclosure

Security vulnerabilities should be reported privately to allow for coordinated disclosure and patching. We follow a 90-day disclosure timeline and credit researchers who report vulnerabilities responsibly.
