Contributors

Learn about the TRANSPOSE community, governance structure, and how to contribute to the project.

Governance

TRANSPOSE follows an open governance model with representation from academic institutions and industry partners. Our steering committee ensures the project maintains its commitment to open science and reproducible research.

Community

Join our growing community of researchers and practitioners working to advance travel demand modeling through open collaboration.

  • GitHub Discussions: Share ideas and ask questions
  • Monthly Community Calls: Connect with other contributors
  • Working Groups: Participate in focused development efforts

How to Contribute

We welcome contributions of all kinds:

  • Code contributions to TDToolkit components
  • Documentation improvements
  • Bug reports and feature requests
  • Dataset contributions (following our data governance guidelines)
  • Benchmark implementations

Please review our Code of Conduct before contributing.

Data Access

TRANSPOSE provides tiered data access:

  • Open Tier: Anonymized, publicly available datasets
  • Research Tier: De-identified data for academic research
  • Restricted Tier: Full datasets with appropriate data use agreements

Publications

Key publications from the TRANSPOSE project:

  • TRANSPOSE: Towards an Open Ecosystem for Travel Demand Modeling (2024)
  • Benchmarking Classical and Machine Learning Models in Travel Demand (2024)
  • ASU-DNN: Hybrid Approaches to Choice Modeling (2023)

Roadmap

Our development priorities for 2024-2025:

  • Q4 2024: Release TDToolkit v1.0 with unified API
  • Q1 2025: Launch community data contribution portal
  • Q2 2025: Implement federated learning capabilities
  • Q3 2025: Expand benchmark suite to include multimodal trips
